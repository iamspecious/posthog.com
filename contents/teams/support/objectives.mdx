## Q3 2026


The big shift this quarter is the switchover from Zendesk to conversations, so every goal below assumes we are working in conversations. SLA attainment will be harder to measure and easy to let slip, so our overriding aim is to protect the customer experience through the transition: hold response times and SLA attainment as steady as we can, and keep CSAT high. Success this quarter looks like customers barely noticing the switchover.


### 1. Improve the proactive and in-app support customers get


<details>
<summary>Surface why a replay won't play (<TeamMember name="Christian Rafferty" photo />)</summary>

- **Rationale:** reduce number of replay tickets by providing more context around our most asked question: "why is my replay not playing / infinitely buffering?"
- **What we'll ship:** our events already carry debug information to be able to answer this. We will use that information and surface it in a way that customers can self-solve rather than needing to raise a ticket.
- **We'll know we're successful when:** fewer "replay won't play" tickets reach the ticket queue.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Build out experiments ticket investigation skill (<TeamMember name="Phillip Ramirez" photo />)</summary>

- **Rationale:** we receive a number of common questions around experiments that we can help customers to self-serve the answers to. The plan here is to reduce the number of these tickets we recieve.
- **What we'll ship:** we'll build a skill that defines how experiments tickets can be investigated. This can be a skill to be used internally and we can even consider making this a customer-facing skill.
- **We'll know we're successful when:** we've shipped at least one troubleshooting skill for experiments and can see those recurring tickets reducing.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Improve the experience of getting support via PostHog AI (<TeamMember name="Christiaan Hendriksen" photo />)</summary>

- **Rationale:** we want customers to receive correct and reliable information via PostHog AI and we want them to be able to seamlessly raise tickets from wherever they are, without having to switch to the PostHog sidebar.
- **What we'll ship:** collect and build data analysis of support quality via PostHog AI. Ship improvements to the way that AI hands-off to support tickets and ship ways for customers to raise tickets via the AI.
- **We'll know we're successful when:** we have a data-backed picture of AI support quality and customers can raise tickets via AI.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Define signals for proactive support (<TeamMember name="Luke Belton" photo />)</summary>

- **Rationale:** if we spot something customers might need help with before they do, we can create a ticket and reach out to them - creating a support experience that sparks joy and prevents churn in a very proactive way.
- **What we'll ship:** define a set of data-backed signals we could use to proactively help customers. This should be based on churn data, information from tickets where customers are getting stuck, changes in customer usage, or ways that we can determine something is not right in the customer's set up. 
- **We'll know we're successful when:** we have a set of concrete signals we could use to create proactive tickets from.

</details>


### 2. Make support faster and smarter in Conversations

<details>
<summary>Bring valuable HogDesk features into Conversations (<TeamMember name="Xander Jones" photo />)</summary>

- **Rationale:** conversations is our new home for support, and as a team using it every day we're well placed to make it better. 
- **What we'll ship:** ship two features we find most valuable in HogDesk and bring them across into Conversations - getting it right for ourselves as much as for our customers.
- **We'll know we're successful when:** our productivity in conversations is improved by these new features

</details>

<details>
<summary>Tidy up the customer support experience and sidebar (<TeamMember name="Ben Lea" photo />)</summary>

- **Rationale:** we want it to be easy for customers to raise tickets with us (from the sidebar, from error messages, as feature feedback), and be able to see what ongoing tickets they have open. At the moment the sidebar is a long, unwieldy scroll of tickets that needs fixing.
- **What we'll ship:** ship changes to the ticket sidebar so that customers don't see all of their tickets at once - resolved tickets can be hidden or available somewhere else. Ship changes so that conversations tickets are raised from error messages and feature feedback.
- **We'll know we're successful when:** customers have a clearer, faster experience raising and following up on tickets.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Better information captured on tickets (<TeamMember name="Kyle Swank" photo />)</summary>

- **Rationale:** the more information we can capture at ticket creation time, the quicker it is for us to investigate a ticket and get solutions to our customers
- **What we'll ship:** we'll create a skill that is invoked when a ticket is created to gather additional information and helps the customer submit a better ticket for us to investigate
- **We'll know we're successful when:** we are receiving better constructed tickets and more immediate context on tickets

</details>

<details>
<summary>Feedback recordings in the Conversations ticket flow (<TeamMember name="Luke Belton" photo />)</summary>

- **What we'll ship:** the ability for customers to submit a recording with their ticket.
- **We'll know we're successful when:** we are receiving tickets from customers with recordings showing us their issues.

</details>

### 3. Billing and legal self-serve

<details>
<summary>Make the BAA process self-serve for startup-plan customers (<TeamMember name="Eleftheria Trivyzaki" photo />)</summary>

- **Rationale:** BAAs require the Boost add-on, but startup credits don’t cover BAAs. Startups on credits shouldn’t be able to sign a BAA in-app without a separate subscription. We also don't currently track BAAs for startup customers or have a defined process, so handling is ad hoc.
- **What we'll ship:** a self-serve path for startup plan customers to request Boost and get a BAA, plus a documented process for handling these requests. Nice to have: visibility into how many existing startup plan customers already have a BAA in place without a separate subscription.
- **We'll know we're successful when:** startup plan customers can resolve the BAA/Boost limitation on their own, we have a repeatable process for handling these requests, and we can report on BAA coverage across the startup plan.

</details>
